About Rusty to the Rescue

"Although Susie is sick in the hospital with a serious illness, the furry snuggles and slobbery kisses provide the most important cure of all...unconditional love."

- From Rusty to the Rescue

Rusty to the Rescue is the heartwarming and sweet story of a little dog named Rusty and his adventures caring for his little girl, Susie.

About the Author

Nestled in the Northern California Valley, surrounded by peach orchards, Katelyn Medlin is a freelance writer with a passion for making children smile. Medlin became interested in writing children's books as a vehicle to tell her own story through the eyes of a child. Her undying love for dogs and her continued commitment to philanthropy have proven to be a perfect match as she crafts her "Rusty" series. Medlin is a full time student working on her Bachelor of Science degree and has also been known to bake a pretty good peach pie.

About Rusty the Dog

"Hi, my name is Rusty and I am 7 years old, and my birthday is October 22, 2008. I weigh 16 pounds and currently live in Yuba City, California with my owners Linda and Terry Brown. At the moment, I am an only dog. Recently I found out about my family tree. I come from a long line of dogs in Germany called the Dachshund family; we are hounds and were bred to hunt and chase animals that burrow.

I don't do much hunting today, but don't let my size fool you; I can keep up with any big dog. In my spare time, I especially love to run, play with a ball and go for bicycle rides in my cart. When I am tired, I nap in the sun or in front of the fireplace. My normal body temperature is 101 so I get cold easily because of my smooth, short, red hair. My eyes are a deep brown color and can be most soulful. I was born with pink toe nails and in my 7 years, I have not seen another dachshund with pink toe nails. I have a large wardrobe of clothes: sweaters, collars, leashes, jackets, even a tuxedo! I try to change up what I wear when visiting shut-ins; at Christmas, my tuxedo gets me lots of attention! Sometimes I get gifts from people I have just met. Last week a 100 year old lady knitted me a cozy blanket. My teeth get brushed every night so my breath does not smell bad when I visit the care homes. My sparkling white teeth seem to make people laugh when Terry shows them off!

My life is not all play; when I was one, I graduated from obedience school and my good manners and sweet nature now enable me to work as a therapy dog through the Delta Corporation. To train, Terry took me everywhere he went so that I would become accustomed to being handled by and talked to by people. Terry and I visit people in care homes, hospitals, and hospice situations in the Yuba-Sutter area. When I was asked to visit a young man who only had four weeks to live, Terry told him to wave at me and when he did, I waved back with my right paw. The man got a belly laugh that lasted the longest time! He was so happy and it made me feel so good to have made him laugh. That was the only time I got to visit him but I will always remember his laughter.

After my back was injured, I was operated on and during my 8 week recovery I was taken to visit my friends in the care homes in a special dog stroller. This put me at the right height to be petted by the people sitting or in bed.

People always seem happy to see me and I really enjoy all the back scratches and pats. Terry and I like to take gifts each week to people in the care homes such as backscratchers, hand lotion, and socks. I get lots of hugs for doing this. If you see me around, just ask me to wave at you and you will laugh too!"

- Rusty
